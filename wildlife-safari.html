Wildlife Safari – Yala

Discover Sri Lanka's most biodiverse national park.

Why Yala?

Yala National Park holds one of the world's highest densities of wild leopards. Its mosaic of scrub, forest, lakes and coastline creates habitats for elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles and over 200 bird species.

Best Time To Visit

  • Feb – Jul: Dryer months, better visibility, higher leopard & elephant sightings.
  • Aug – Jan: Greener landscape, migratory birds.

Safari Tips

  • Choose dawn or late-afternoon drives for active wildlife.
  • Hire an experienced tracker guide (interpret behavior & spot cryptic species).
  • Carry binoculars & telephoto lens (300mm+ for birds).
  • Stay at least 2–3 nights to improve sighting odds.

Conservation Etiquette

Maintain distance, avoid loud noises & never feed animals. Responsible tourism protects fragile ecosystems.

Combine With

Visit Bundala National Park for water birds or relax on the beaches near Kirinda & Tangalle post-safari.

At A Glance

  • Region: Southeast Sri Lanka
  • Nearest Town: Tissamaharama
  • Access: 6h drive from Colombo
  • Key Species: Leopard, Elephant, Sloth Bear
  • Bird Count: 200+ species
